Z-Loss
Auxiliary loss term
Lz = ε · log²(Z)
Added to the main cross-entropy loss.
ε is a small constant (e.g. 1e-4) — just a nudge, not a hard constraint.
Why log²(Z)?
When Z = 1, log(Z) = 0 → zero penalty. When Z grows large, log²(Z) penalises it quadratically — strongly discouraging large partition functions.
Effect
The model learns to keep logits small so Z stays near 1. Softmax denominator stays well-behaved throughout training.
In practice: Z-loss adds almost zero compute overhead and significantly reduces loss spikes in large-scale training. Used in PaLM and other Google models.
