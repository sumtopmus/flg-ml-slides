Positional Embeddings — RoPE
Key insight: Rotate query and key vectors by angles proportional to their position. The dot product then depends only on relative distance, not absolute positions.
How
Rotation by angle θ·m
Token at position m is rotated by angle θ·m. Applied to Q and K vectors, not at the input embeddings.
Why it works
Relative angle in dot product
(RotQ) · (RotK)ᵀ collapses to angle θ·(m−n) — only the relative distance between tokens survives.
Effect
Distance-aware attention
Far tokens → large rotation diff → smaller dot product. Nearby tokens attend more strongly. Position encoded geometrically.
Rotation applied at position m
qm = R(θ·m) · q
kn = R(θ·n) · k
Dot product result
qm · kn = f(q, k, m−n)

Only relative position (m−n) matters
