RMS Norm
Layer Norm (original)
μ = mean(x)
σ² = var(x)
y = (x − μ) / √(σ² + ε) · γ + β
— centers AND scales
RMS Norm (modern)
RMS(x) = √( mean(x²) )

y = x / RMS(x) · γ
— scales only, no mean-centering
Simpler
Removes mean subtraction and the β bias term — fewer operations per forward pass.
Faster
LayerNorm needs two statistics: mean(x) then variance. RMSNorm only needs mean(x²) — one pass, no mean-centering step.
Same quality
Empirically matches LayerNorm on large LLMs. Used in LLaMA, Mistral, Gemma, and most modern models.
