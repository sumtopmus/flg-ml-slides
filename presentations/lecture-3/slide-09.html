Softmax is the Problem
softmax(xᵢ) = exp(xᵢ) / Z    where    Z = Σ exp(xⱼ)
— Z is called the partition function
Large logits → overflow
If any xᵢ is large, exp(xᵢ) overflows to ∞. The entire softmax becomes NaN.
Large Z → bad gradients
When Z ≫ 1 the softmax is very flat. Gradients become tiny → vanishing gradient. Training stalls.
Goal: keep Z ≈ 1
If Z stays close to 1, the distribution is well-normalised and gradients flow cleanly through the softmax.
