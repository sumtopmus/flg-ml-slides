Regularization at Pretraining
Skip
Dropout
Not used during pretraining.

Pretraining makes a single pass over a massive corpus — the dataset itself is so large that overfitting is not a concern, making dropout unnecessary.
Still used
Weight Decay
Still applied during pretraining — but not for regularization.

Used because it produces better training losses and leads to more stable optimization. The regularization effect is a side benefit, not the goal.
Summary: At pretraining scale, the corpus size acts as the regularizer. Dropout would just slow training. Weight decay is kept for its effect on the loss landscape.
