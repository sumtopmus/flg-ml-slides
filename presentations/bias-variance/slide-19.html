§ 4 — Estimation & Practice

Approximating 𝔼D[·] — four approaches

Bootstrap
Resample to simulate 𝔼D[·]
  1. Draw B bootstrap samples ≈ different datasets D.
  2. Fit model on each: f̂₁(x)…f̂B(x).
  3. Bias of f̂b ≈ 𝔼D[f̂D]; Variance of f̂b ≈ Var[f̂].
Cross-Validation
Estimates MSE
  • CV estimates total MSE = Bias² + Var + noise.
  • Each fold is like a mini-simulation of "what if I had a somewhat different training set."
  • You just select "which model has the lowest total error."
    A model with too much bias or too much variance will score poorly.
Simulation
Compute 𝔼D[·] exactly
Only possible when true f(x) is known.
Generate M datasets; fit on each; predict at fixed x*.
Sample mean → 𝔼D[f̂]; sample variance → Var[f̂].
Analytical
Known formulas for some models
Linear models: 𝔼D[·] is tractable in closed form.
Estimate σ² = RSS/(n−p) from residuals.
